    Atrophy and Stiffening Analysis

    Ocular Motility Lab @ UCLA

    August 2019 - December 2019

    Background

    Beta-zone peripapillary atrophy (PPA) is commonly observed with glaucoma and vision loss. Given the findings of adduction-induced deformation of the peripapillary retina and optic disc, we sought to determine the potential correlation between PPA and magnitude of deformation in patients with primary open angle glaucoma and statistically normal intraocular pressure.

    Project

    I first assisted in scanning subjects using the optical coherence tomography (OCT) scanner with a specific protocol, and conducted literature reviews on peripapillary atrophy (specifically beta-zone atrophy in glaucoma research). I assisted with manual segmentation of beta-zone peripapillary atrophy and the analyses for blood vessel deformation using Adobe Photoshop.

    The abstract (1st Author) describing this study and its results were accepted to ARVO 2020 and published; link to the abstract is provided below. The actual paper (2nd Author) is currently under review by IOVS.
