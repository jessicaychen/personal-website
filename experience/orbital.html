    Orbital Fat Volume Analysis

    Ocular Motility Lab @ UCLA

    August 2019 - December 2019

    Background

    Patients with primary open angle glaucoma (POAG) are commonly treated with topical prostaglandin analogs (PGAs) in order to reduce intraocular pressure (IOP). These eye drops reportedly cause orbital fat atrophy, resulting in sunken eyes. The purpose of this study was to determine the effects of PGAs on POAG patients without statistically elevated IOP, as well as determine whether orbital dimensions itself may be related to these cases.

    Project

    Using ImageJ, I manually segmented MRI stacks of the orbit (outer tracing as shown in the image) and the elements inside the orbit, including the optic nerve (center), extraocular muscles (5 oblong shapes within the orbit), and blood vessels (smaller tracings). In order to determine the orbital volume, the areas of the elements within the orbit are subtracted from the orbital area and multiplied by the image plane thickness; this analysis was done between two stationary anatomical references. I ran statistical analyses on the gathered data to determine differences in orbital dimensions and orbital fat volume between POAG subjects and controls using IBM SPSS and graphed the results in GraphPad Prism.

    A thorough description of this study and its results are published; link to the article is provided below.
