    En Face Image Analysis

    Ocular Motility Lab @ UCLA

    January 2020

    Background

    In angles of adduction (looking towards nose), the optic nerve is known to become tethered, causing deformation to the peripapillary retina, which can be represented by the blood vessels attached to the peripapillary retina. The purpose of this study was to measure the magnitude of deformation in adduction and abduction (looking towards the temples) using markers on the blood vessel fidicuals and analyze the differences in change between younger and older subjects.

    Project

    This project had been sent out for publication by the time I joined the lab; my role in this project involved re-analyzing images using Adobe Photoshop and the developed protocol for revisions and further cross-checking. This project served as an introduction into biomedical image analysis with optical coherence tomography en face scans, automated analyses, and statistical analyses.

    A thorough description of this study and its results are published; link to the article is provided below.
